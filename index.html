Introduction

Hong Kong is a wonderful place with huge diversity in almost anything.

History

In 1840, Britain attacked northern China; this attack was known as the First Opium War. To make peace, China agreed to cede Hong Kong Island to Britain in 1841. Hong Kong became part of the British empire until 1997, when the lease on the New Territories expired and the entire city was handed back to China. In 1984, after lengthy negotiations, Hong Kong was signed off and handed over to China. From there Hong Kong grew and transformed into a commercial and finanical hub.

Geography

Hong Kong is located in Asia, clingling to the southern tip of China's Guangdong Province and bordering the city of Shenzhen. The whole Hong Kong area amounts to merely 426 square miles making it one of the most densely populated cities in the world. The four major geographical land areas in Hong Kong are Hong Kong Island, Kowloon, The New Territories, and the Outlying Island.

    Festival

    Chinese New Year Festival.

    Every year on February 10, Chinese New Year is celebrated all around Hong Kong with food, lights, and dragon festivals.

    Language

    Spoken language: Cantonese

    Cantonese is the main language spoken in Hong Kong. When it comes to writing, Hong Kong uses traditional chinese characters; however the grammar is the same for both speaking and writing the language.

    Wedding Clothing

    The bride and groom's traditional wedding clothing in the Chinese culture

    For traditional wedding events, the husband and wife will wear these formal ceremonial clothing. The red coloring in the clothing represents happiness, fortune, and prosperity. The bride's dress is called a Qipao and groom's clothing is called a Tang Suit.

    Hong Kong Flag

    A Photo of Hong Kong's Flag

    The Hong Kong flag was designed to be a symbol of unity. Its circular arrangment of the white flower pedals set against a red background supposedly emodies the rights and freedoms. The flower is called the Bauhinia Blakeana, and the five pedals and stars reflect the five major regions of China.

    Music

    The Erhu.

    The Erhu is a bowed instrument played in Hong Kong as part of the Chinese culture. There are two strings on the instrument. It is sometimes referred to as a Chinese violin or fiddle in the western world. The instrument is used in theactrical plays and movies.

    Street Markets

    Night Time Markets in Hong Kong

    Every day and night, the street markets are full of people buying, bidding, and selling merchanise. It can get a little crazy in the streets when things heat up, but it's a great experience to sight see.
